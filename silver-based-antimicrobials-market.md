<p><p>The silver-based antimicrobials market offers different types of carriers for delivering the antimicrobial properties. Silicate carriers are made from silicon compounds and are effective in controlling microbial growth. Phosphate carriers are made from phosphate compounds and provide sustained release of silver ions, reducing bacterial growth. Titanium dioxide carriers use titanium dioxide as a carrier material to release silver ions, which have strong antimicrobial properties. Finally, glass carriers contain silver nanoparticles embedded in a glass matrix, which slowly release silver ions, inhibiting microbial growth. These carriers play a crucial role in enhancing the effectiveness of silver-based antimicrobials.</p></p>
