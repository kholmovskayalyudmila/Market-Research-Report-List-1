<p><p>Wood chips are classified into two main market types: soft wood chips and hard wood chips. Soft wood chips are derived from coniferous trees, which have needle-like leaves and produce softer wood. They are commonly used for manufacturing paper and pulp products due to their long fibers and high cellulose content. On the other hand, hard wood chips come from deciduous trees, which have broad leaves and produce harder wood. They are typically used in industries such as particleboard manufacturing, bioenergy, and landscaping.</p></p>
