<p><p>The specialty gases for the semiconductor market include various types such as nitrogen trifluoride, tungsten hexafluoride, hydrogen chloride, ammonia, and others. Nitrogen trifluoride is used for cleaning and etching purposes in the semiconductor manufacturing process. Tungsten hexafluoride is utilized as a dopant gas in the production of integrated circuits. Hydrogen chloride is used for plasma etching and cleaning applications. Ammonia is employed for the deposition of dielectric films. Other specialty gases like silane, methane, oxygen, and carbon dioxide serve different purposes in the semiconductor industry, such as doping, oxidation, and cleaning.</p></p>
