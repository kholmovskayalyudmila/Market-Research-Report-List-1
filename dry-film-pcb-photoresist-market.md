<p><p>Dry Film PCB (Printed Circuit Board) Photoresist is a type of photosensitive material used in the fabrication of printed circuit boards. It is applied as a thin film on the surface of the PCB and acts as a light-sensitive mask during the etching process. The photoresist film is exposed to UV light through a photomask, and areas that are not exposed are protected, while exposed regions are chemically developed, creating a pattern that defines the circuitry on the board.</p><p>The future outlook for the Dry Film PCB Photoresist Market is expected to be positive, with a projected growth rate of 5% during the forecasted period. <p><p>The Dry Film PCB Photoresist market offers two types: Positive and Negative. Positive photoresist is a type of photoresist that becomes soluble in a developer when exposed to light, leaving behind the desired pattern on the surface. It is commonly used for high-resolution applications. On the other hand, Negative photoresist becomes insoluble in a developer when exposed to light, resulting in a protective layer on the surface where it was applied. This type is often used when high contrast is required or for photolithography processes. Both types cater to different application needs in the PCB industry.</p></p>
