<p><p>Ethylene-Propylene-Diene Monomer (EPDM) is a high-density synthetic rubber primarily composed of ethylene, propylene, and a diene monomer. It possesses excellent resistance to heat, weathering, ozone, and aging, making it suitable for various applications such as automotive parts, electrical insulation, roofing, and seals.</p><p>The EPDM market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The demand for EPDM is largely driven by its increasing use in the automotive industry for the production of vehicle parts like door seals, gaskets, and hoses. The automotive sector is experiencing rapid growth globally, particularly in emerging economies. This, coupled with stringent regulations regarding automotive emissions and fuel efficiency, is propelling the demand for EPDM in the sector.</p><p>Another key driver of the EPDM market is the rise in construction activities worldwide. EPDM is widely used in roofing membranes, waterproofing systems, and sealants due to its durability and ability to withstand extreme weather conditions. The growth in the construction industry, driven by urbanization and infrastructure development, is fueling the demand for EPDM.</p><p>Moreover, EPDM finds applications in electrical insulation, synthetic rubber products, and wire and cable coatings. The growing electrical and electronics sector, along with the increasing need for energy-efficient products, is anticipated to drive the demand for EPDM.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, Asia Pacific is expected to dominate the EPDM market due to the presence of major automotive and construction industries in countries like China, India, and Japan.
