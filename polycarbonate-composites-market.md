<p><p>The polycarbonate composites market is divided into two types: glass fiber filled and carbon fiber filled. Glass fiber filled polycarbonate composites are made by adding glass fibers to the polycarbonate material, enhancing its strength, stiffness, and impact resistance. These composites are often used in applications that require high mechanical performance, such as automotive parts, electrical connectors, and sporting goods. Carbon fiber filled polycarbonate composites, on the other hand, are made by incorporating carbon fibers into the polycarbonate material, offering excellent strength, light weight, and heat resistance. These composites find application in aerospace, automotive, and other industries that demand high-performance materials.</p></p>
