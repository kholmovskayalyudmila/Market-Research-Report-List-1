<p><p>Extruded graphite is a high-quality synthetic material that is produced by mixing petroleum coke and coal tar pitch. The mixture is then extruded and shaped into various forms, such as rods, blocks, and electrodes, using a high-temperature process. Extruded graphite possesses excellent electrical conductivity, thermal resistance, chemical stability, and low ash content, making it an ideal material for a wide range of applications.</p><p>The global extruded graphite market has witnessed significant growth over the years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period. The market is primarily driven by the increasing demand for carbon-based materials in crucial industries, such as automotive, aerospace, and electronics. Extruded graphite finds extensive usage in these sectors due to its superior properties, including high thermal conductivity, low coefficient of friction, and excellent resistance to oxidation and corrosion.</p><p>Moreover, the growing emphasis on sustainable and renewable energy sources is also contributing to the market's growth. Extruded graphite is widely used in the manufacturing of lithium-ion batteries, fuel cells, and solar cells, which are essential components of renewable energy systems. The increasing adoption of electric vehicles and the rising installations of solar energy projects further boost the demand for extruded graphite.</p><p>In terms of regional analysis, Asia Pacific dominates the extruded graphite market due to rapid industrialization, urbanization, and infrastructure development in countries like China, India, and Japan. <p><p>The extruded graphite market consists of various types: pitch impregnated, un-impregnated, round, and square. Square graphite, as the name suggests, has a square shape and is preferred for specific uses.
