<p><p>The concrete slab market consists of two main types: hollow slabs and solid slabs. Hollow slabs are characterized by their inner voids, resulting in a lighter weight and reduced material usage. They are commonly used in construction projects where weight is a concern, such as high-rise buildings. On the other hand, solid slabs have no voids and provide greater structural strength. They are often preferred in projects that require heavy loads, such as industrial buildings or parking garages. Both types cater to different construction needs, offering unique advantages based on the intended usage.</p></p>
