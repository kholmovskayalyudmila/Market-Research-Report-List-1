<p><p>Electrical silicon steel is a type of steel used in the manufacturing of transformers, motors, and generators due to its excellent magnetic properties. It is available in two types: low silicon and high silicon. Low silicon electrical steel has a lower silicon content, typically less than 3.5%, resulting in higher magnetic induction and lower core losses. On the other hand, high silicon electrical steel has a higher silicon content, typically between 3.5% and 4.8%, offering lower coercivity and hysteresis losses while maintaining good magnetic permeability. These different market types cater to specific applications and performance requirements in the electrical industry.</p></p>
