<p><p>An automotive rear-view camera, also known as a backup camera, is a safety feature that provides drivers with a clear view of the area behind their vehicle while reversing. It typically consists of a wide-angle camera mounted at the rear of the vehicle, connected to a display screen on the dashboard or rear-view mirror. The camera captures real-time footage, improving visibility and helping drivers avoid collisions with obstacles or pedestrians.</p><p>The future of the automotive rear-view camera market looks promising. With increasing concerns about road safety, governments across the globe are implementing regulations mandating the installation of rear-view cameras in new vehicles. For example, in the United States, the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) issued a final rule in 2014 requiring all new cars and light trucks to have rear-view visibility systems by May 2018. Such regulations are driving the demand for automotive rear-view cameras.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology are enhancing the capabilities of rear-view cameras. Manufacturers are focusing on developing cameras with higher resolution, wider viewing angles, and additional features like night vision and object detection. Additionally, the integration of rear-view cameras with advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and vehicle telematics is further fueling market growth.</p><p>The current outlook of the automotive rear-view camera market is positive. According to the mentioned statement, it is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.9% during the forecast period. Factors such as the increasing number of road accidents and fatalities, the rising demand for convenient parking systems, and the growing adoption of electric vehicles are driving the market growth. Moreover, the decreasing cost of cameras and the rising consumer awareness regarding safety features are expected to further boost the demand for automotive rear-view cameras in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>The automotive rear-view camera market comprises three main types: exterior mirrors, interior mirrors, and under rearview mirrors. Exterior mirrors are traditional side mirrors that provide a visual aid for the driver to see behind and beside the vehicle. Interior mirrors are typically located in the cabin and provide a clear view of the rear through the windshield. Under rearview mirrors are cameras installed under the rear of the vehicle, allowing the driver to see the surroundings on a display screen. These types of cameras enhance visibility and help prevent accidents.</p></p>
