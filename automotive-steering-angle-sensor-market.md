<p><p>The automotive steering angle sensor market is divided into two main types: digital sensors and analog sensors. Digital sensors use binary code to provide precise and accurate steering angle measurements, allowing for better control and responsiveness of the vehicle. Analog sensors, on the other hand, provide continuous and variable voltage readings that represent the steering angle. Although analog sensors may be less accurate than digital sensors, they are less expensive and still provide a reliable indication of the steering angle for most automotive applications.</p></p>
