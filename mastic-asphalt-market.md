<p><p>Mastic asphalt is a type of asphalt that is commonly used in road construction and roofing applications. There are two main types of mastic asphalt: standard and polymer modified. </p><p>Standard mastic asphalt is a traditional form of asphalt that is made by heating bitumen and combining it with fine aggregates. It is known for its durability, water resistance, and ease of application. Standard mastic asphalt is commonly used in road surfacing, bridge decks, and car parks.</p><p>Polymer modified mastic asphalt, on the other hand, is a more advanced form of asphalt that is blended with polymers during the manufacturing process. This modification enhances its properties, making it even more durable, flexible, and resistant to cracking. Polymer modified mastic asphalt is often used in high-stress areas such as airports, highways, and industrial flooring.</p></p>
