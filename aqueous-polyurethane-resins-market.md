<p><p>Aqueous polyurethane resins belong to the family of polyurethane materials that are dispersed or emulsified in water. The two main types are aqueous polyurethane dispersions (PUDs) and aqueous polyurethane emulsions. PUDs are obtained by dispersing polyurethane particles in water, while emulsions are formed by mixing polyurethane with water using an emulsifying agent. Both types offer various advantages like low volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions, environmental friendliness, excellent adhesion, and resistance to chemicals, making them widely used in industries such as coatings, adhesives, textiles, and leather applications.</p></p>
