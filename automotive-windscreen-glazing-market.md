<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The automotive windscreen glazing market comprises three main types: laminated glass, tempered glass, and polycarbonate. Laminated glass is constructed using two layers of glass with an interlayer of polyvinyl butyral (PVB) in between, which provides enhanced safety as it prevents the glass from shattering upon impact. Tempered glass is made by heating and cooling regular glass quickly, making it stronger and safer as it breaks into small, dull pieces when shattered. Polycarbonate is a lightweight, durable, and impact-resistant type of plastic used as an alternative to glass in windshields.</p></p>
