<p><p>Dielectric materials are widely used in various industries, such as electronics, telecommunications, and automotive, for their insulating properties. The market for dielectric materials includes different types, namely calcium titanate, magnesium titanate, barium titanate, and others. Calcium titanate offers excellent dielectric properties, high temperature stability, and low loss, making it suitable for high-power applications. Magnesium titanate is known for its resistance to temperature changes and low dielectric constant, making it preferred in radio frequency devices. Barium titanate is widely used due to its high dielectric constant and piezoelectric properties. The "other" category includes various dielectric materials with unique characteristics for different application needs.</p></p>
