<p><p>Oil tanker cargo ships are classified into various market types based on their size and capacity. Very Large Crude Carriers (VLCCs) are tankers with a capacity between 200,000 and 320,000 deadweight tons (DWT) and are typically used for long-haul crude oil transportation. Ultra Large Crude Carriers (ULCCs) are even larger, with a capacity above 320,000 DWT. ULCCs are used for transporting massive quantities of crude oil over long distances, often requiring deep-water ports due to their size. These market types represent different segments within the oil tanker industry, catering to specific transportation needs.</p></p>
