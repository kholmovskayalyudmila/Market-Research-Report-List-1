<p><p>Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR) refers to techniques used to extract additional oil from existing oil fields, increasing their productivity. The three main types of EOR techniques are gas injection, thermal injection, and chemical injection. Gas injection involves injecting gases, such as carbon dioxide or nitrogen, into the reservoir to reduce the oil's viscosity and enhance its flow. Thermal injection utilizes steam or hot water to heat the oil, making it easier to pump out. Chemical injection involves injecting chemicals to reduce the oil's surface tension, allowing it to flow more easily.</p></p>
