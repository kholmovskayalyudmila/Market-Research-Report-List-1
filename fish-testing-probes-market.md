<p><p>FISH (Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization) testing probes are used in genetic and molecular research to visually identify specific DNA sequences in cells. There are three types of FISH testing probes: single color, dual color, and multi-color. </p><p>In single color FISH, only one probe is used to target a particular DNA sequence, making the identification process simpler and faster. </p><p>Dual color FISH involves using two different probes labeled with different fluorescent dyes, allowing researchers to identify two different DNA sequences or detect genetic rearrangements.</p><p>Multi-color FISH employs more than two probes, each labeled with a different fluorescent dye in order to simultaneously detect multiple DNA sequences or specific chromosome abnormalities. This technique provides researchers with a comprehensive view of genetic information within cells.</p></p>
