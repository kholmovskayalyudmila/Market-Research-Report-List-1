<p><p>The Automotive Engine Electric System market consists of two types of electrical wires: primary and secondary. Primary electrical wires are responsible for carrying high voltage current from the battery to the ignition system, fuel injectors, and other components. They are designed to handle high temperatures and ensure efficient electrical flow. On the other hand, secondary electrical wires transmit lower voltage current from the ignition system to the spark plugs. These wires are insulated to prevent electrical interference and ensure accurate timing for spark plug firing. Both types are crucial for the proper functioning of the engine's electrical system.</p></p>
