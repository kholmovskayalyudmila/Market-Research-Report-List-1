<p><p>Pulp logs can be classified into two market types: Type I and Type II. Type I refers to logs that are suitable for pulping purposes, typically harvested from fast-growing trees specifically grown for this purpose. These logs have consistent quality and characteristics that make them ideal for pulp and paper production. Type II refers to logs that are not grown specifically for pulping but are still used in the industry due to their availability, lower cost, and acceptable fiber quality. Type II logs may come from different tree species or may be recovered from various sources like residues or thinnings.</p></p>
