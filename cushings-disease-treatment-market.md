<p><p>Cushing's disease is a rare and serious medical condition that occurs when the body is exposed to high levels of the hormone cortisol for an extended period. It is commonly caused by a benign tumor in the pituitary gland, which stimulates the production of excessive cortisol. Cushing's disease treatment aims to reduce cortisol levels and alleviate symptoms.</p><p>The current treatment options for Cushing's disease include surgery, medication, and radiation therapy. Transsphenoidal surgery is often the primary choice, involving the removal of the tumor through the nasal cavity. If surgery is not feasible or unsuccessful, medications like ketoconazole or mifepristone may be prescribed to inhibit cortisol production. In some cases, radiation therapy is used to destroy tumor cells and control cortisol levels.</p><p>The future outlook of the Cushing's disease treatment market appears promising.
