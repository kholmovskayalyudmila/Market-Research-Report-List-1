<p><ul><li>Glued</li><li>Floating</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Multi-layer parquet is a type of flooring that consists of multiple layers of wood. Glued parquet refers to the installation method where the individual pieces are glued directly to the subfloor. This provides a stable and durable result. Floating parquet, on the other hand, is installed without the use of glue or nails. The planks are fitted together with a locking mechanism, allowing them to "float" above the subfloor. This type of installation is popular for its ease and flexibility, as it can be easily removed or replaced.</p></p>
