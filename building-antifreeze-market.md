<p><p>The building antifreeze market consists of various types, including ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and others. Ethylene glycol is a widely used antifreeze due to its ability to prevent freezing and overheating in building systems. Propylene glycol, although similar to ethylene glycol, offers a safer and less toxic alternative. Both glycol types are commonly used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems. The "others" category includes alternative antifreeze options such as glycerin or alcohol-based solutions that also provide protection against freezing and overheating in buildings.</p></p>
