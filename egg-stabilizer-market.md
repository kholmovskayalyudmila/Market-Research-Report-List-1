<p><p>The egg stabilizer market consists of various types of stabilizers, including cream of tartar, sugar, and starch. Cream of tartar is a natural stabilizer derived from grapes and helps in stabilizing egg whites for consistency and volume in recipes like meringues. Sugar acts as a stabilizer by absorbing moisture and providing a stable structure to baked goods with eggs. Starch, such as cornstarch or potato starch, also functions as a stabilizer by thickening egg-based mixtures and preventing separation or curdling. These stabilizers play a crucial role in achieving desired texture and stability in dishes using eggs.</p></p>
