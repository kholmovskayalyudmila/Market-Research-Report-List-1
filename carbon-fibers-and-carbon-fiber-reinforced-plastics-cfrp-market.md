Similarly, the aerospace industry is transitioning towards CFRP for aircraft manufacturing to achieve lightweight structures, leading to reduced fuel consumption.</p><p>In addition, there is a rising trend of renewable energy sources, such as wind energy, which further boosts the demand for CFRP. PAN-based carbon fiber is produced from polyacrylonitrile and accounts for the majority of the market. Pitch-based carbon fiber is derived from coal tar or petroleum pitch and is known for its high strength. Lastly, carbon fibers based on other precursors are produced from alternative sources such as rayon or lignin. These materials are used to manufacture carbon fiber reinforced plastics (CFRP), which are composite materials consisting of carbon fibers embedded in a polymer matrix.</p></p>
