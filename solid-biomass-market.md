<p><p>The solid biomass market is classified into various types, including wood pellets, crop residue, energy crops, municipal solid waste (MSW), and others. Wood pellets are made from compressed sawdust and are commonly used as a renewable fuel source. Crop residue refers to the leftover material after the harvest, such as corn stalks or wheat straw, which can be converted into energy. Energy crops are specifically grown for biomass production, like switchgrass or miscanthus. Municipal solid waste (MSW) includes household garbage and waste from businesses, which can be processed for energy generation. Lastly, "others" refer to any other type of solid biomass not mentioned above.</p></p>
