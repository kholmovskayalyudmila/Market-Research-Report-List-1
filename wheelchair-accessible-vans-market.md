<p><p>The Wheelchair Accessible Vans market consists of two main types: Side Entry and Rear Entry. Side Entry vans have ramps or lifts installed on the side of the vehicle, allowing wheelchair users to enter and exit through the side doors. These vans offer more versatile seating options and are generally preferred by individuals who prefer to sit in the front passenger seat or drive their own vehicle. On the other hand, Rear Entry vans have ramps or lifts installed at the rear of the vehicle, providing easier access for wheelchair users to enter and exit from the back of the van. These vans are commonly used in taxi or transport services, as they allow multiple wheelchair passengers to be accommodated simultaneously.</p></p>
