<p><p>Battery isolators are devices used in vehicles to separate the starting battery from auxiliary batteries to prevent draining the primary power source when the vehicle is not in use. Diode type isolators use diodes to create a one-way pathway for current, allowing power flow from the alternator to the batteries. Relay type isolators utilize electromagnetic relays to control the flow of current between batteries. Both types offer efficient power management, ensuring that each battery is charged and isolated when needed, depending on the vehicle's electrical system requirements.</p></p>
