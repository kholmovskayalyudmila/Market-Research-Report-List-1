<p><p>Chitin, a natural polysaccharide, is categorized into two main market types: Food Grade Chitin and Industrial Grade Chitin. Food Grade Chitin refers to the purified form that is typically derived from crustacean shells and is used in various food applications due to its ability to act as a thickening agent, stabilizer, or coating. Industrial Grade Chitin, on the other hand, is used in non-food applications like cosmetics, agriculture, biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and wastewater treatment due to its unique properties such as biocompatibility, biodegradability, and antimicrobial activity.</p></p>
