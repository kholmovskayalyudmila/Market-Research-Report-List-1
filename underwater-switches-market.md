<p><p>Underwater switches refer to a specific type of switches designed to function in underwater environments. There are two main types of underwater switches available in the market, namely limit switches and proximity switches. Limit switches are devices that detect the presence or absence of an object by physical contact, whereas proximity switches rely on non-contact methods, such as sensing changes in electromagnetic fields or using infrared radiation, to detect objects. Both types of switches are commonly used in underwater applications, providing reliable control and automation in underwater systems.</p></p>
