<p><p>Acrylic polymer emulsion adhesives are classified into different types based on their modifications. Silicone modification refers to the addition of silicone compounds to enhance the adhesive's flexibility and resistance to heat and moisture. Epoxy modification involves incorporating epoxy resins to improve the adhesive's durability, chemical resistance, and bonding strength. Other modifications may include the addition of additives or crosslinkers to enhance specific properties of the adhesive, such as UV resistance or water resistance. These modifications enable the adhesives to be tailored for various applications and meet different performance requirements.</p></p>
