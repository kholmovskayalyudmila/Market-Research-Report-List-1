<p><p>Amalgam filling is a common dental restoration material used to fill cavities. Two types are low-copper amalgam and high-copper amalgam. Low-copper amalgam is a traditional blend of silver, tin, and mercury. It has a long-standing track record but is less durable. On the other hand, high-copper amalgam contains more copper and less silver, resulting in improved strength and longevity. It offers better resistance to corrosion and fractures, making it a preferred choice for dental restorations. Both types have their pros and cons, but high-copper amalgam is considered more advanced and reliable.</p></p>
