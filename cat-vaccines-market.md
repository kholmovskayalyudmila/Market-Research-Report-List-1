<p><strong>The Cat Vaccines Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Attenuated Live Vaccines</li><li>Conjugate Vaccines</li><li>Inactivated Vaccines</li><li>Subunit Vaccines</li><li>Toxoid Vaccines</li><li>DNA Vaccines</li><li>Recombinant Vaccines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cat vaccines are classified into several types based on their composition and method of production. Attenuated live vaccines contain weakened versions of the virus or bacteria to stimulate an immune response. Conjugate vaccines combine antigens with carrier proteins to enhance effectiveness. Inactivated vaccines use killed or inactivated pathogens to trigger immunity. Subunit vaccines contain only specific antigens from the pathogen. Toxoid vaccines are made from inactivated toxins, and DNA vaccines use genetic material to produce antigens. Recombinant vaccines involve using genetically engineered organisms to produce specific antigens. These different types offer various options for protecting cats from infectious diseases.</p></p>
