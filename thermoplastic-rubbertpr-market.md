<p><p>Thermoplastic Rubber (TPR) is a type of synthetic rubber that can be melted and reshaped multiple times without losing its properties. There are several types of TPR in the market. Styrene-based TPE (SBCs) are widely used for their excellent flexibility and resilience. Thermoplastic Polyolefins are known for their durability and resistance to chemicals. Thermoplastic Polyurethanes offer high abrasion resistance and flexibility. Polyether Ester TPE (TPEE) exhibit high heat resistance and are commonly used in automotive applications. Other types of TPR include Silicone-based TPE and Hybrid TPE, which offer specific properties based on their composition and application.</p></p>
