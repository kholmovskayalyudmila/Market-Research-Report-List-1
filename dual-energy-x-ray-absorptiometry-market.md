<p><p>Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DEXA) is a diagnostic technique used to measure bone density and analyze body composition. The market for DEXA consists of two types: axial bone densitometers and peripheral bone densitometers. Axial bone densitometers are primarily used to assess the density of the spine and hip, while peripheral bone densitometers are designed for measuring bone density in peripheral regions like the wrist, finger, or heel. Both types of DEXA systems provide accurate and non-invasive assessments, aiding in the diagnosis and monitoring of osteoporosis and other conditions related to bone health.</p></p>
