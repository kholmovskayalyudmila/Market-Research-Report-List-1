<p><p>Subsea pipeline jumpers refer to short sections of pipe that connect two larger pipeline segments underwater. There are two main types: rigid and flexible jumpers. Rigid jumpers are made of strong materials like steel and are suitable for connecting pipelines that experience high pressure and temperature fluctuations. On the other hand, flexible jumpers are composed of more pliable materials like composite or elastomeric materials, allowing for greater flexibility and movement. These jumpers cater to pipelines that require more flexibility due to geological or operational considerations.</p></p>
