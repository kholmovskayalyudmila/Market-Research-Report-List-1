<p><p>Paints and coatings rheology modifiers are substances added to improve the flow and consistency of paint formulations. Organic rheology modifiers are derived from natural sources, such as cellulose or starch, and enhance the viscosity and suspension properties of paints. Inorganic rheology modifiers, on the other hand, are minerals or synthetic materials, like clay or silica, which improve the thixotropic behavior and sag resistance of coatings. Both types of modifiers play a crucial role in achieving desired texture, application characteristics, and overall performance of paints and coatings.</p></p>
